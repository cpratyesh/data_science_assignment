## Quick summary of findings

The headline result that kept coming up: **Fear days are actually better for these traders, not worse.** Higher PnL, more trades, and a stronger long bias — all during days when the broader market is scared. It points to a pretty sophisticated contrarian approach from at least a portion of this trader base.

Segmenting accounts into 3 clusters (Consistent Winners, High Frequency, Cautious Traders) helped explain a lot. The Consistent Winners barely change their behavior across sentiment regimes — they seem to just run a system regardless of what the index says. The Cautious Traders do the worst thing possible: pull back on Fear days when opportunity is highest.

Tried logistic regression first as a baseline (61%), then switched to Random Forest which got to 70% — good enough given the dataset size shows today's behavior is a decent predictor of tomorrow's profitability. Sentiment matters but it's not the strongest signal — recent PnL and win rate are more predictive.

Full reasoning and strategy recommendations are in `writeup.md`.
